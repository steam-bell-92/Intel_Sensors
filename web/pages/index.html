Why These Sensors?

Intel Berkeley Research Lab deployed 54 sensors gathering environmental signals. Their combined patterns hint at human presence even without explicit labels.

Signals Captured

  • Temperature drift tracks human heat signatures.
  • Humidity spikes mirror collective respiration.
  • Light variance reveals workspace activity.
  • Voltage stability exposes sensor health.

Noise Is Expected

Bit-flip noise and missing packets appear in realistic deployments. Our models embrace that messiness rather than hiding it.

Occupancy Labels

Labels are inferred, not provided. The workflow aligns raw sensor combinations with actual room usage through careful feature engineering.

Safety-First Tradeoff

Missing a real occupancy event is far costlier than false alarms. The pipeline prioritizes recall to support high-stakes environments.

🔥

Hazard response systems demand aggressive detection thresholds.

👴

Elder care monitoring calls for minimal false negatives.

🏢

Secure zones benefit from proactive alerts over silent failures.
